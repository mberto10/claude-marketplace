## Category Notes

### Foundational vs. Applied
- **Foundational**: cognitive-transformation-graphs, mid-chain-commoditization, illegibility-gradient
- **Applied**: skills-as-modules, provenance-architecture, human-ai-interface-design, compound-loop

The applied ideas are downstream of the foundational ones. You need the lenses (graphs, commoditization, illegibility) before you can design the implementations (skills, interfaces, loops).

Note: **illegibility-gradient** is now foundational - it's not derived from other ideas but rather extends and completes the framework.

### Descriptive vs. Prescriptive
- **Descriptive** (what's happening): mid-chain-commoditization, execution-vaporization, illegibility-gradient (the gradient itself)
- **Prescriptive** (what to do about it): membrane-model, human-ai-interface-design, skills-as-modules, compound-loop, anti-complexity-engineering, illegibility-gradient (the strategic implications)

The illegibility-gradient is both - it describes the structure of knowledge AND prescribes strategy (compound what you can; cultivate what you can't).

### Theory vs. Case Study
- **Theory** (my framework): cognitive-transformation-graphs, mid-chain-commoditization, value-at-boundaries, membrane-model, illegibility-gradient
- **Case Study** (validates theory): compound-loop, compound-engineering-parallels

### Two Strategic Logics (NEW)

The framework now contains two complementary strategies mapped to the illegibility gradient:

**Compound Logic (Zones 1-2: Specifiable)**
- Skills-as-modules
- Compound-loop
- Anti-complexity-engineering
- Strategy: Document, package, iterate, accumulate

**Cultivation Logic (Zones 3-4: Irreducible)**
- The Five Irreducibles (from illegibility-gradient)
- Membrane functions
- Strategy: Experience, relationships, taste development

**Meta-strategy:** Know which zone you're in. Apply the right logic.
